  • INCLUSION

    Inclusion is the attitude, trend or policy of integrating all people into society, with the aim that they can participate and contribute to it and benefit from this process. The word, as such, comes from the Latin inclusio, inclusiōnis. Inclusion seeks to ensure that all individuals or social groups, especially those who are in conditions of segregation or marginalization, may have the same possibilities and opportunities to fulfill themselves as individuals. Educational inclusion is a concept of pedagogy. It states that the school must be able to include all individuals in the educational process, regardless of their condition, origin, race, religion or gender, etc. For school inclusion, diversity is a positive value within the school. Understand that we are all different and that, regardless of our characteristics (physical, psychological, social, cultural), we have the same right to receive a quality education.

  • PIAR

    Decree 1421 of 2017, which regulates the care of the population with disability, defines the Individual Plan for Reasonable Accommodations (PIAR) as a “tool used to guarantee the teaching and learning processes of students, based on pedagogical and social assessment, which includes the supports and reasonable adjustments required, including curricular ones, of infrastructure and all others necessary to guarantee learning, participation, permanence and promotion. It is an input for classroom planning of the respective teacher and the Institutional Improvement Plan (PMI), as complement to the transformations made based on the Universal Design for Learning (DUA)

  • Entry criteria

    The criteria for admission to the Institution, are established in accordance with the provisions of the SED, MEN and the conditions individuals that the institution has for provide the student with inclusion, defined in the present document: • That in response to the evaluation pedagogy meets certain criteria minimum of: • Functional communication level, effective and generalizable • Basic cognitive skills that allow to be placed in a grade in the whose chronological age is not higher three years from the average age of the grade • With coexistence skills that allow you to interact in a group, without highly problematic behaviors challenging; such as aggressiveness, hyperactivity and excessive restlessness affect their integrity or that of the group school. • That the student does not require support physical, human and/or technical character substantial with which the institution. • Have students present all the specific medical documentation, which certify your disability status. • When the referral is internal, follow the procedure stated in the protocol of attention given by School Guidance and specialized support. • That the family know and accept the current regulations of the program inclusion embodied in the Manual of Coexistence; committing to fulfill the duties set forth in said document.

  • Permanence criteria

    Regarding the permanence of the students included in the regular classroom, it is done necessary to establish specific criteria that allow clarity on why and even when a student with a disability will be included in the classroom. Therefore, the student and family must comply with the following requirements:
    • Achieve the objectives proposed for each period, according to the settings reasonable (PIAR, Decree 1421 of 2017). • Demonstrate positive attitudes and motivation for school activities in general • Demonstrate through different channels some cognitive and social progress, in the academic competencies. • Show adaptation to school; living positively with their classmates and teachers. • Regularly attend the Institution, justifying the absences in writing. • The permanence and continuity in the basic secondary education, will depend of each particular case, which will be analyzed in the evaluation commissions • Participation and family commitment in terms of: • Specialized medical care and therapeutics for the health sector • Support in school activities in home
